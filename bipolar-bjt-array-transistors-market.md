<p><p>The Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT) array market includes both NPN and PNP transistor types, each serving distinct applications. NPN transistors, which allow current to flow from collector to emitter when a positive voltage is applied to the base, are commonly used in switching and amplification roles. PNP transistors, on the other hand, conduct when the base is at a lower voltage than the emitter, often utilized in complementary circuits. The market caters to diverse industries like consumer electronics, automotive, and telecommunications.</p></p>
