<p><p>The short-wave infrared (SWIR) camera market is primarily divided into area cameras and linear cameras. Area cameras capture two-dimensional images across a wide field of view, making them suitable for applications like surveillance and industrial monitoring. In contrast, linear cameras are designed to capture one-dimensional data, scanning objects linearly, which is ideal for applications such as production line inspection and hyperspectral imaging. Both types leverage SWIR technology for enhanced imaging capabilities in challenging lighting conditions.</p></p>
