<p><p>Umbilical cord blood banking involves the collection and storage of stem cells from umbilical cord blood after childbirth. There are two primary types: public and private cord blood banks. Public cord blood banks collect and store donations that are available for anyone in need, often used for research and transplants. In contrast, private cord blood banks store the stem cells exclusively for the family’s future medical use, which can be more expensive and may not be suitable for everyone.</p></p>
