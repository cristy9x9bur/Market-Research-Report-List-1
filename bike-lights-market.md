<p><p>The bike lights market is primarily divided into two categories: headlights and taillights. Headlights are designed to illuminate the path ahead, enhancing visibility while riding during low-light conditions, and ensuring safety from obstacles and vehicular traffic. Taillights, on the other hand, are crucial for signaling the cyclist’s presence to drivers behind them, often featuring red LEDs that make the rider more visible from a distance. Both types play essential roles in cyclist safety and are increasingly equipped with advanced features like flashing modes and rechargeable batteries.</p></p>
